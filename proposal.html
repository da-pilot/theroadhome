01 · Executive Review

Enterprise technology.
Zero cost.

Adobe proposes a no-cost website uplift for The Road Home, migrating theroadhome.com from WordPress to Adobe Experience Manager (AEM). This tri-party partnership delivers enterprise-grade technology to TRH while enabling Adobe to showcase next-generation tools in a real-world nonprofit context.

This partnership creates mutual value: TRH receives enterprise technology at no cost, Adobe gains real-world insights for product development, Modelic receives training on Adobe content management technologies.

What TRH Gets
  • Modern, SEO-optimized platform with better search rankings (Core Web Vitals + GEO)
  • Document-based content management requiring no technical expertise
  • Zero migration and hosting costs through Adobe's community licensing
What's Required from TRH
  • Validate content and commit to 2-month go-live post-approval
  • Maintain ongoing content and share feedback with Adobe
  • Participate in a shared Microsoft Teams channel with all parties
02 · Roles & Responsibilities

Who does what

Three parties, three clear lanes. Each entity has defined responsibilities so the engagement stays focused and on-schedule.

01 · Technology partner
Adobe
Providing the platform, at no cost
  • Performs the full site uplift — a light UX modernization from WordPress to AEM
  • Covers all migration and on-going hosting costs via Adobe's community licensing program
  • Delivers 3× 1-hour authoring training sessions for TRH staff during handoff
  • Augments Modelic staff for the first six months post-launch
  • Maintains a shared Microsoft Teams channel for ongoing support questions
02 · Client
The Road Home
Content owner & feedback source
  • Reviews and validates all uplifted content, making edits as necessary
  • Commits to go-live within two months after prototype approval
  • Manages ongoing content maintenance after launch
  • Shares product feedback with Adobe to support continued learning and improvement
  • Participates in shared Teams channel for run-the-business questions
03 · Code partner
Modelic
Long-term code stewardship
  • Receives 3× 2-hour in-person AEM developer training sessions from Adobe
  • Responsible for all ongoing code maintenance after handoff
  • Supported by Adobe augmentation staff for the first six months
  • Participates in shared Teams channel for technical coordination
03 · Key Drivers

Why this, why now

Three concrete outcomes that make this uplift worth doing — not aspirational, measurable.

01

Better SEO & GEO

The new site will achieve passing Core Web Vitals scores, now a direct signal for search ranking. It will also be automatically optimized for AI-based citations and searches (Generative Engine Optimization), positioning TRH in front of people seeking help through tools like ChatGPT and Perplexity.

02

Low-effort modernization

The goal is uplift, not reinvention. The new site delivers a refreshed visitor experience — cleaner layout, faster load times, improved accessibility — without requiring heavy investment from TRH or disrupting the site's proven information architecture.

03

Document-based authoring

Adobe Experience Manager gives TRH staff a Microsoft Word or Google Docs-style editing experience. No CMS training required, no developer dependency for routine content updates. Any non-technical team member can publish and edit pages independently.
