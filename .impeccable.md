# Design Personality — The Road Home redesign

## References I like

**Community / human-first nonprofits** — Partners In Health, Habitat for Humanity's newer properties, World Central Kitchen's post-2022 site. The shared traits I want to carry:

- Named portraits, not anonymous hands
- Magazine-style typographic rhythm, not dashboard grids
- Empathetic without being saccharine — human dignity as the organizing principle, not pity

## Things that annoy me

Two failure modes to actively avoid:

1. **Stock nonprofit clichés.** No hands-holding-hands. No outstretched palms. No sepia filters. No "together we can" hero copy. No generic stock photography that could belong to any of a hundred 501(c)(3)s.
2. **Too-clever startup design.** No huge aurora gradients, no floating glass shapes, no ML-buzzword register, no oversize emoji or animated mesh backgrounds. This is a 100-year-old homeless-services org, not a Y Combinator alum.

Downstream skills should reject both directions on sight.

## One rule I want bent

**"Every hero needs a giant CTA button."** The current site has the teal DONATE slab competing with the headline, a second DONATE in the nav, and several more below the fold. That's dashboard behavior, not service-first behavior.

**Bend:** Let the hero be a quiet statement — a dignified portrait and a single sentence of context. No button in the hero block itself. DONATE lives in the sticky top nav and reappears lower in the page at a natural moment (e.g., after an impact number), not stacked with the headline.

**Why:** a person in crisis scanning the page should not have to parse four competing CTAs. A donor scrolling casually still finds DONATE where it belongs — in the header, always — and is given a reason to act by the time they reach the ask.

## Implications for downstream skills

- **Wireframes / prototype:** hero without a button. Sticky top nav with DONATE. Two-lane page (crisis-path and donor-path) that stay visually distinct without fighting each other.
- **Copy:** keep "Refuge. Resources. Relief." — it's already the right voice. Replace WordPress filler. Use the existing phone-number-as-CTA pattern in crisis sections.
- **Photography:** real, named subjects. Documentary, not hopeful-golden-hour. Salt Lake City context visible.
- **Typography:** preserve the HarmoniaSans / DINNextLTPro register (or faithful modern substitutes — Inter Display / Inter is acceptable, DM Sans / IBM Plex Sans also acceptable). No serif display. No script.
- **Palette:** teal family only. No warm accent added. Cream and pink presets from the WordPress theme are not part of the real brand — ignore them.
- **Motion:** restrained. Quiet fades and gentle rises. No parallax, no scroll-jacking, no auto-playing video heroes.
- **Responsive:** every prototype is responsive from the start — desktop (≥1200px), tablet (≤900px), and mobile (≤520px) breakpoints, driven by `:root` token overrides inside `@media` blocks so the type scale and spacing scale together. Nav links collapse to a horizontal-scroll strip on tablet; utility bar drops secondary items on mobile. **No prototype is considered complete if it only renders at 1440px.**
