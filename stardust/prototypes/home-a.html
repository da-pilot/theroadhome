A story from Palmer Court

The hardest thing about being unhoused wasn't sleeping outside. It was the paperwork. The Road Home sat with me through every form.
Darnell H.   Permanent supportive housing, 4 years

Darnell moved into Palmer Court in 2022, after eight months in and out of emergency shelter. His caseworker, Theresa, helped him get his ID replaced, enroll in Medicaid, and find a part-time job at a hardware store in Sugar House.

Today he's working toward a CDL. He wants to drive for a delivery company. And he says the thing he never expected about being housed is how much he sleeps — real sleep, not shelter sleep.
