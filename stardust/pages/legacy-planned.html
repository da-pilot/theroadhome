Give · Legacy & Planned Giving

Begin your legacy with us.

A planned gift is a gift that keeps helping our neighbors long after you have moved on. Most planned gifts cost nothing today — they simply direct where a portion of your estate will go when the time comes.

Bequest language

If you are ready to add The Road Home to your will or trust, share this language with your estate attorney:

"I give, devise, and bequeath to The Road Home, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization with EIN 87-0212465, the sum of $______ (or _____ % of my residuary estate) to be used for its general charitable purposes."
TipResiduary bequests — a percentage of what's left after other gifts — tend to keep their value as your estate changes over time.

Types of planned gifts

Beyond a simple bequest, several other vehicles let you give in ways that also make sense for your estate plan. A few of the most common:

  • Retirement account beneficiary — name The Road Home on an IRA or 401(k) for potential tax advantages to your heirs.
  • Life insurance — designate The Road Home as a beneficiary, full or partial.
  • Charitable remainder trust — receive income during your lifetime; the remainder supports the mission.
  • Real property — donate a home, land, or other real estate.
  • Donor-advised fund — recommend grants to The Road Home from an existing DAF.
