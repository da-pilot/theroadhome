Donations

Does The Road Home accept clothing, diapers, shoes, etc.?

Yes, we accept any of these new or gently used at any of our resource centers, 7 days a week, between 8:00am – 7:00pm.

  • Connie Crosby Family Resource Center (529 West 9th Avenue, about 7300 South): Please deliver to the warehouse on the West side.
  • Pamela Atkinson Men's Resource Center (3380 South 1000 West): Turn right at the first entrance near the black Parking sign.
  • Gail Miller Resource Center (242 Paramount Ave., Salt Lake City): Pull up to the gate, click the call button on the black panel.

What is the tax ID / EIN for The Road Home?

The Road Home is a 501(c)(3) charitable organization. Our federal ID number is 87-0212465. All donations are tax-deductible to the extent allowed by law.

Can I donate stock, real property, or plan a bequest?

Yes. See our Give hub for every giving method, or contact Holly Rogers at hrogers@theroadhome.org or 801-819-7294.

Is my donation of $10 or less accepted online?

For security purposes, donations of $10 or less must be made by phone at 801-819-7294.

Volunteering

How can I help?

Visit our Get Involved page. Opportunities include weekly and one-time commitments, item drives, Eagle Scout projects, internships, and group activities.

Can my company volunteer as a group?

Yes. Email volunteer@theroadhome.org with team size and dates you're considering. We'll set up an orientation and match you with an activity.

Can I volunteer while serving a court-ordered community-service requirement?

Yes — we participate in court-ordered community service. See our Court-Ordered Volunteering page for specific requirements and the application process.

Is there an age requirement?

Weekly activities generally require volunteers to be 16 or older. One-time group activities and drives have lower age minimums depending on the specific role. Family-friendly opportunities are listed on the volunteer calendar.

Shelter & services

Are the resource centers closed during the day?

Our resource centers are open 365 days a year for intake, donation drop-off, and services. Shelter sleeping areas are reset during daytime hours while residents attend programming, school, or work.

What resources are available at the resource centers?

Beds, meals, case management, behavioral-health support, employment assistance, housing navigation, and help accessing benefits like Medicaid, SNAP, and ID replacement.

Do I need an ID to get shelter?

No. We can help you replace your ID as part of intake. We do not turn anyone away for lack of paperwork.

I am doing a school project or paper. Who can I reach out to?

Email media-center@theroadhome.org or visit our Media Center page.

Community

I saw someone outside who needs help. What should I do?

In non-emergency weather, call the Homeless Services Line at 801-990-9999. In dangerous weather or a medical emergency, call 911. Our outreach teams respond to non-emergency calls.

Can I tour a resource center or Palmer Court?

Tours are available to donors, prospective volunteers, and community partners by appointment. Email info@theroadhome.org to schedule.

How can I stay informed about homelessness policy in Utah?

Sign up for our policy newsletter. We send short, specific updates — never more than once a month.
