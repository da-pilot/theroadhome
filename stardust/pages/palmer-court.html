Program · Palmer Court

201 homes.
201 neighbors.

Palmer Court is a 201-unit permanent supportive housing complex operated by The Road Home. Residents at Palmer Court have previously experienced long-term homelessness.

A home, not a shelter.

Palmer Court is proof that housing is the solution to homelessness. Every resident has their own apartment and access to on-site case management, behavioral-health support, and employment assistance. No time limit. No "graduation" requirement.

The building is not a shelter. It is a home.

Residents are individuals and families who have experienced chronic homelessness — often combined with disability, serious mental illness, or substance use. Palmer Court meets them where they are.

Palmer Court opened in 2009 and remains one of the most successful examples of the Housing First model in the western United States. Annual retention rates are above 90%.

Volunteer with us

Ways to be part of Palmer Court.

Palmer Court welcomes volunteers into programming that strengthens the community within the building. Weekly, monthly, and one-time opportunities.

Weekly · kids

Literacy Night · Kids' Book Club

Help spark interest in reading with children ages 5–12. One-hour weekly commitment. Ages 16+. Orientation and background check required; three-month minimum.

One-time · group

Group activities

Design your own project, host a drive, or plan a holiday event. We are flexible and try to accommodate ideas. Groups sign Boundaries & Confidentiality forms.

Seasonal

Holidays

Many ways for groups to help during the holiday season — collecting donations, organizing holiday activities, delivering move-in kits to new families.

Schedule your activity with our Volunteer Coordinators: volunteer@theroadhome.org · 385-351-2389.
