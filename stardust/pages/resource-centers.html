Get Help · Resource Centers

Three resource centers. One mission.

Walk in, call, or get referred. The Road Home's resource centers are the physical entry points to every program we run — from a bed tonight to a housing plan tomorrow.

What is a resource center?

Not just a shelter.

A resource center is not a shelter, and it is not just a shelter. It is a day-and-night building with intake staff, case managers, hot food, and a path to housing — all under one roof.

Each of our three centers is open 365 days a year for intake, donation drop-off, and services. Sleeping areas are reset during daytime hours while residents attend programming, school, or work.

The building you walk into determines which population you are served with (single men, families, donation drop-off) — but every center has the same mission and the same network of case managers and housing navigators behind it.

What to bring

Come as you are.

We do not require paperwork at intake. If you have what's on the list, it helps — but it is never a reason we turn anyone away. What we need most is a conversation.

If you have medications or a service animal, please mention either at intake so we can plan accordingly.

Helpful, not requiredAn ID if you have one. We can help you replace a lost ID as part of intake.
Medication for 24 hoursWhatever you need tonight and tomorrow morning. We can help refill longer prescriptions.
Important documentsBirth certificate, Social Security card, any housing or legal paperwork.
Service animals welcomePlease note: other pets cannot be housed in the resource center.
