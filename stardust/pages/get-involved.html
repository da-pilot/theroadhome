Pillar · Get Involved

When we work together, we can build a stronger community.

The Road Home relies on volunteers to provide programs and services to individuals and families working to overcome homelessness. There are many ways to show up — as an individual, a family, a team, or a company.

Moments that matter

What a week looks like.

Short notes from the resource centers — staff, guests, and volunteers making something happen.

Cory

Opening up, slowly.

Cory had been staying in emergency shelter for multiple years and was hesitant to engage in supportive services. Then a conversation about housing options opened the door.

Jordan

Communication is key.

Case managers at the Men's Resource Center hosted a "Communication is Key" workshop to help with communication skills and boundary-setting.

Sharon

A family back on track.

Sharon, a case manager at the Midvale Family Resource Center, worked with a family whose previous barriers included eviction history and credit. Now housed.

The Nelsons

Home for the holidays.

After staying at the Midvale Family Resource Center for a while, the Nelson family successfully navigated into permanent housing before the holidays.
