Get Help · Emergency Shelter

Shelter is often the first step on the road home.

The Road Home operates low-barrier shelter with housing-focused services for men and families in Salt Lake County, Utah.

801-990-999924 hr Homeless Services Line

A bed, a meal, a plan.

Our Emergency Shelters, the South Salt Lake Men's Resource Center and the Midvale Family Resource Center, are open 365 days a year and serve people experiencing homelessness in Salt Lake County.

Along with providing life-saving emergency shelter, we help people re-build their support networks and create stability through supportive services and housing placement.

If you are in need of shelter please contact Utah Community Action's Homeless Services Line at 801-990-9999.

What to expect

Three steps from first call to first morning.

Everyone's story is different, but the first 24 hours usually looks like this.

01

Call or walk in

Homeless Services Line 24 hours, or in-person intake at either resource center. Interpreters available.

02

Meet with a case manager

Same day if possible. We listen, we ask about immediate needs, and we do not require paperwork you don't have.

03

Bed. Meal. Plan.

Shelter that night. Housing pathway the next day. Wrap-around support starts immediately.
