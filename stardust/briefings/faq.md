## Q&A (questions verbatim, answers verbatim where present + synthesized where live site has them collapsed)

### Donations
**Q: Does The Road Home accept clothing, diapers, shoes, etc.?**
A (verbatim): Yes, we accept any of these new or gently used at any of our resource centers, 7 days a week, between 8:00am – 7:00pm.
- **Connie Crosby Family Resource Center** (529 West 9th Avenue, about 7300 South): Please deliver to the warehouse on the West side.
- **Pamela Atkinson Men's Resource Center** (3380 South 1000 West): Turn right at the first entrance near the black Parking sign.
- **Gail Miller Resource Center** (242 Paramount Ave., Salt Lake City): Pull up to the gate, click the call button on the black panel.
Click here to see our full needs list.

**Q: What is the tax ID / EIN for The Road Home?**
A: The Road Home is a 501(c)(3) charitable organization. Our federal ID number is **87-0212465**.

**Q: Can I donate stock, real property, or plan a bequest?**
A: Yes. See our [Give](/give-main/) hub for every giving method, or contact Holly Rogers at **hrogers@theroadhome.org** or 801-819-7294.

### Volunteering
**Q: How can I help?**
A: Visit our [Get Involved](/get-involved/) page. Opportunities include weekly and one-time commitments, item drives, Eagle Scout projects, internships, and group activities.

**Q: Can my company volunteer as a group?**
A: Yes. Email volunteer@theroadhome.org with team size and dates.

**Q: Can I volunteer while serving a court-ordered community-service requirement?**
A: Yes — see our Court-Ordered Volunteering page for requirements.

### Shelter & services
**Q: Are the resource centers closed during the day?**
A (synthesized): Our resource centers are open 365 days a year for intake, donation drop-off, and services. Shelter sleeping areas are reset during daytime hours while residents attend programming, school, or work.

**Q: What resources are available at the resource centers?**
A: Beds, meals, case management, behavioral health support, employment assistance, housing navigation, and help accessing benefits like Medicaid, SNAP, and ID replacement.

**Q: I am doing a school project or paper. Who can I reach out to?**
A: Email media-center@theroadhome.org or visit our Media Center page.

### Community
**Q: I saw someone outside who needs help. What should I do?**
A: In non-emergency weather, call the Homeless Services Line at **801-990-9999**. In dangerous weather or a medical emergency, call **911**. Our outreach teams respond to non-emergency calls.
