03 · Voice

Voice

Service-first and plainspoken. Lead with an action the reader can take now. Use uppercase display for slogans only — do not shout in body copy. Two audiences, two registers: instructional for someone in crisis, brisk and accountable for donors.

Direct Service-first Calm Plainspoken Numeric when it matters
Do

REFUGE. RESOURCES. RELIEF.

Looking for immediate shelter? Call 801-990-9999.

On any given night, we have over 1,550 people in housing, in the community.

Don't

Join us on our journey to end the crisis of unhoused communities.

Together, we can make a difference in the lives of those less fortunate.

Make an impact today by donating to our important cause.

Hard rules

  1. Lead with the action — call this number, find a shelter.
  2. Uppercase is for slogans only. Never shout in body copy.
  3. Prefer triplets: three one-beat sentences separated by periods, like "Refuge. Resources. Relief."
  4. State scale in concrete numbers, not adjectives.
  5. One register for the crisis audience (instructional), one for donors (brisk, accountable). Keep them separate on the page.

06 · Photography

Photography direction

Do

  • Real people, real settings — clients, staff, volunteers, with names when possible.
  • Dignified subjects, composed, clothed for the weather. Jackets, beanies, warmth.
  • Salt Lake City context visible — architecture, mountains, recognizable streets.
  • Natural light, muted cool tones — let the brand teal stay the loudest color.
  • Room for a centered white display headline overlay.

Don't

  • Sepia hardship clichés or pity framing.
  • Faces of clients shown in distress or shame.
  • Stock photography that could belong to any nonprofit.
  • Hands-holding-hands, outstretched palms, or prayer-circle compositions.
  • Golden-hour uplift that pretends the problem is solved.
