## Executive review
Adobe proposes a no-cost website uplift for The Road Home, migrating theroadhome.com from WordPress to Adobe Experience Manager (AEM). This tri-party partnership delivers enterprise-grade technology to TRH while enabling Adobe to showcase next-generation tools in a real-world nonprofit context.

### What TRH Gets
- Modern, SEO-optimized platform with better search rankings (Core Web Vitals + GEO)
- Document-based content management requiring no technical expertise
- Zero migration and hosting costs through Adobe's community licensing

### What's Required
- TRH validates content and commits to 2-month go-live post-approval
- Modelic handles ongoing code maintenance with Adobe's initial support
- All parties share learnings through a collaborative Teams channel

This partnership creates mutual value: TRH receives enterprise technology at no cost, Adobe gains real-world insights for product development, Modelic receives training on Adobe content management technologies.

## Roles & Responsibilities
### Adobe
Adobe will do an uplift of theroadhome.com. An uplift is a light UX modernization of the existing site. The site would move from Wordpress to the next-generation of Adobe Experience Manager (AEM), Adobe’s content management solution. At the customer's discretion, the existing information architecture (site structure) can be left as-is or revisited.

All services are provided free of charge as a part of Adobe's ongoing VIP program to learn from real customers how they use Adobe's solutions. On-going hosting costs will also be provided as part of Adobe's community licensing program.

Adobe would provide 3x 1-hour authoring training sessions for TRH staff during the handoff period. Similarly, Adobe would provide training for Modelic for any future code updates necessary. All three entities would have a shared Microsoft Teams channel for on-going / "run the business" questions.

### The Road Home
If the proposal is accepted, The Road Home would be responsible for validating the uplifted content, and making any edits as necessary. The Road Home would also agree to go live within two months after prototype approval to ensure Adobe can learn from The Road Home's experiences.

The Road Home would be responsible for on-going content maintenance and sharing feedback with Adobe about the products and services provided.

### Modelic
Adobe would train Modelic on the technologies used to make the new TRH site. This would be provided as 3x 2-hour in-person training sessions. Modelic would be responsible for on-going code maintenance, while Adobe would augment Modelic staff for the first six months.

## Key drivers
1. Better SEO & GEO - The new site would have passing "Core Web Vitals" which has become a signal for better search rankings. The new site would also be automatically optimized for AI-based citations and searches (GEO).
2. Low-effort modernization - The new site would have a refreshed visitor experience without being a radical departure. The goal is to "uplift" without requiring heavy investment from all three parties.
3. Adobe Experience Manager provides a "document-based" authoring experience that allows any non-technical person to make updates to their site as if they were using Microsoft Word or Google Docs.
